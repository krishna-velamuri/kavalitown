Place: Udayagiri
Distance: 80KM
Rating: 4.00
Best Time: Sep - Feb

Udayagiri fort built by Vijayanagara kings in the 14th century. A towering peak reaching a height of 994m (3,261 ft) can be seen here. Some people will call this hill as Sanjiva hill because a wide variery of madicinal herbs grow on it. The fort is located on the hill and 3 are below. It covers total 7km area. It was the stronghold of the Gajapathis of Orissa till Krishna devaraya annexed it. During gajapathis and Vinjayangara rulers, the town grew to great splendor. The ruins of 365 Temples found in the town reflected the sculpture and architecture of various periods.

Place: Bhairava Kona
Distance: 90KM
Rating: 4.00
Best Time: Sep - Feb

This piligrim centre is located in Ambavaram Kothapalli village, CS purma mandal of Prakasam district. It is located border of Nellore, Kadapa and Prakasam districts. Thousands of people from the three districts visit the place to witness the spectacle and also take a bathe in the waterfall and worship lord Shiva and his consort on the auspicious day.

Historians and Archaeologists have researched and concluded that this temple dates back to the period between 7th and 8th century A.D. The rock cut cave temples are somewhat semilar to the one at Mahabhalipuram, not surprising because the cave temples at Bhairavakona were built around the 7th century A.D probably by Vishnukundina kings, in the time period as the Pallava kings of Mahabalipuram. There are 8 shrines for Lord Shiva in 8 different forms. The sculpture also reminds you the sculpture at Mahabalipuram.

Bhairava kona draws thousands of people from far and near for a bath on the occasion of Karthika pournami and Sivarathri. An interesting feature that attracts devotees to Bhairavakona is that moonlight falls on the idol of goddess Durgadevi on the day of Karthika pournami. Breathtaking scenary all around , the jaw dropping waterfalls, chirping of the birds and the wildlife all around all add the glory of this place.
